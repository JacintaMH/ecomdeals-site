Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: May 2025

Plain English Summary

Some links on EcomDeals earn us a commission if you click and make a purchase. This never affects the price you pay, and it never influences which tools we recommend.

How We Make Money

EcomDeals participates in affiliate marketing programmes. This means that when you click certain links on our site and go on to sign up for or purchase a product or service, we may receive a commission from the company you purchase from.

These commissions are paid by the companies whose products we feature — not by you. You will never pay more by using our links, and in many cases you'll pay less thanks to the exclusive deals we negotiate.

Which Links Are Affiliate Links

Any link on this site that leads to a third-party product or service may be an affiliate link. We don't label every single link individually, but we do clearly mark deals and discount codes as affiliate offers throughout the site. If you're ever unsure, you can assume that any "Get Deal" button or link to a third-party platform is an affiliate link.

Our Commitment to You

We only feature products and services that we genuinely believe are useful to ecommerce founders. We do not accept payment to feature a product, and affiliate relationships do not influence our editorial content or blog posts.

If we wouldn't recommend something to a friend building an ecommerce business, it doesn't appear on EcomDeals — regardless of whether a commission is available.

Compliance

This disclosure is made in accordance with the UK Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) guidelines, the Committee of Advertising Practice (CAP) Code, and the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008.
