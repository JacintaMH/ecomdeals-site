If you're running paid ads on Meta, TikTok, or Google and relying solely on platform-reported ROAS, you're making decisions based on numbers that are almost certainly wrong. Every platform takes credit for every sale it can — and they all overclaim.

Third-party attribution tools exist to give you a single, honest view of what's actually driving revenue. Triple Whale and Northbeam are the two most talked-about options for Shopify brands right now. Here's how they actually compare.

## What Each Tool Is Built For

**Triple Whale** was built specifically for direct-to-consumer Shopify brands. It pulls your Shopify revenue, ad spend, and creative performance into one dashboard — the "Moby" summary screen gives you a daily snapshot of your key metrics in under a minute. It's designed to be accessible to founders and marketers who aren't data analysts.

**Northbeam** was built for brands with more complex, multi-channel ad strategies and larger budgets. It offers more granular attribution modelling and is generally favoured by performance marketing teams who want to dig deep into channel-level data rather than get a clean daily summary.

## Attribution Modelling: How They Handle the Hard Problem

Both tools use a combination of pixel tracking, first-party data, and statistical modelling to attribute revenue when cookies and iOS privacy changes make direct tracking unreliable.

Triple Whale uses its own **Pixel** (installed on your Shopify store) alongside a post-purchase survey to triangulate attribution. The survey asks customers directly how they found you — surprisingly powerful data that no algorithm can replicate.

Northbeam leans more heavily on **multi-touch modelling** and algorithmic attribution. It's more sophisticated under the hood but requires more setup time and a larger volume of data to become accurate. Smaller brands won't get reliable results until they're spending consistently at scale.

> **Worth noting:** Neither tool is perfectly accurate — that's not a flaw, it's the nature of modern attribution. What you're buying is a more reliable signal than Meta Ads Manager, not a ground truth.

## Ease of Use

This is where Triple Whale wins clearly. The dashboard is clean, the onboarding is fast, and the daily summary format means you can make decisions quickly without needing a data background. It's built for the founder checking numbers at 7am.

Northbeam is more powerful but noticeably more complex. Expect a longer setup, more configuration, and a steeper learning curve. It rewards teams who will actually use the advanced features — if you just want clean daily reporting, it's overkill.

## Creative Analytics

Triple Whale has invested heavily in **creative reporting** — you can see which ad creatives are driving revenue, not just clicks. For brands running a lot of Meta or TikTok creative testing, this is genuinely useful and saves time consolidating data manually.

Northbeam's creative reporting is more limited. Its strength is channel-level and campaign-level attribution, not creative performance. If creative testing is central to your strategy, Triple Whale has the edge here.

According to [Shopify's commerce trends data](https://www.shopify.com/research/future-of-commerce), first-party data strategies are now essential for scaling brands as third-party tracking continues to erode — both tools are built around this reality, but Triple Whale makes it more actionable for smaller teams.

## Which Brands Should Use Each

A useful rule of thumb: if you're spending under £50k/month on ads and don't have a dedicated data analyst, Triple Whale will give you more value faster. If you're above that threshold, running complex multi-channel strategies, and have someone who can properly configure and interpret the data, Northbeam's depth justifies the cost.

[Meta's own guidance on conversions API](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/marketing-api/conversions-api) makes clear that server-side tracking is increasingly necessary — both Triple Whale and Northbeam support this, but setup complexity differs significantly between them.

## Our Verdict

> Triple Whale for most Shopify brands; Northbeam for scaling teams with the budget and resource to use it properly.

- **Choose Triple Whale if** you're a founder or small team running Meta and TikTok ads and want fast, clean daily reporting without a data analyst
- **Choose Triple Whale if** creative performance visibility matters to your decision-making
- **Choose Northbeam if** you're spending £50k+ per month across multiple channels and have a performance marketing team to configure and act on the data
