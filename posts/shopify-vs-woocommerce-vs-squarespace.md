Choosing your ecommerce platform is one of the most important decisions you'll make as an online store owner. Get it right and you have a solid foundation to scale from. Get it wrong and you're looking at a painful, expensive migration six months down the line.

We've broken down the three most popular options — Shopify, WooCommerce, and Squarespace — across the factors that actually matter.

## Who Each Platform is Built For

**Shopify** was built from the ground up for ecommerce. It's the default choice for serious online stores because everything — from checkout to inventory to shipping integrations — is designed specifically for selling. If you're planning to scale, run ads, or manage a large product catalogue, Shopify is almost always the right answer.

**WooCommerce** is a plugin for WordPress, making it the most flexible option on this list. You can build almost anything with it, but that flexibility comes with a cost — you're responsible for hosting, security, updates, and plugin compatibility. It's best suited to businesses that already have a WordPress site or have developer resource available.

**Squarespace** is built for aesthetics first, commerce second. If your products are visually driven — jewellery, art, photography, fashion — and you care deeply about how your store looks, Squarespace gives you the most polished templates out of the box.

## Our Verdict

- **Choose Shopify** if you're serious about ecommerce growth, running paid ads, or managing more than 50 products.
- **Choose WooCommerce** if you already have WordPress, want full customisation, or have developer support.
- **Choose Squarespace** if your store is small, visually-led, and you value design over functionality.
